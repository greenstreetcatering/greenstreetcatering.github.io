Beginnings

Started in 2010, we've been busy. Please enjoy a snapshot of what keeps us busy.

  • October 23, 2010

    Discovering a need.

    Green Street was started in 2010 by a group of friends, comfortable in the kitchen, desiring to use their gifts and abilities to provide weekly meals to anyone in need. The program began after the economy had recently taken a downturn and the need for a substantial, warm meal was becoming more and more prevalent. Green Street was initially modeled after a ministry that had been running for many years through a church in NC. After much research and lots of prayer, the friends were confident that a similar program would benefit the High Country. Since then, Green Street Catering has been adapting to the unique needs of the community as they grow and change.

  • Summer 2011

    Neighbors helping neighbors

    Green Street's first meals were served on October 23, 2010. 42 meals were prepared and distributed from the kitchen at Alliance Bible Fellowship Church in Boone, North Carolina. Many who joined for dinner were requesting additional meals for others, unable to make it to the facility themselves. This began the beauty of people helping people. Over the years, hundreds of community members have become Green Street volunteers by distributing meals and allowing this program to have an even farther reach and benefit. Green Street has served over 70,000 meals since 2010 and the very nature of our organization creates a space for anyone who is willing to take part in serving each other. Green Street Catering is built upon neighbors helping neigbors.

  • July 2013

    A catering company.

    Just as individuals/families are helped by meals, Green Street became aware of many other churches, service organizations and non-profits that are burdened by food costs and food preparation. This started our work to assist and cater for company/group gatherings so as to curb costs and allow them to focus on accomplishing their specific missions. We began catering events as well as assiting other service organizations to provide fresh food availability to the communities they serve. Green Street desires the success of all community servants. In this partnership through food, Green Street offers service organizations a tool to strengthen their own programs and purposes. These partnerships have also allowed us the honor and privledge to serve school communities, church groups, hospice patients, those with special needs, veterans, victims of abuse, foster children and families, those with disabilities, sports teams, homeless individuals and individuals recovering from addiction. We cater from small individually plated meals to large buffet affairs. As we all come together, we create stronger individuals and communities.

Nutrition Education

Green Street strives to increase the overall wellness and nutrition of the people we serve. This program educates and equips individuals to begin and maintain a healthy lifestyle first by providing tip sheets monthly with food that is distributed. These tip sheets offer financial advice for smart shopping and emphasize the importance of adequate nutrition to live a healthier lifestyle. The NEED program also hosts at least two classes per year on concepts such as healthy cooking strategies and shopping for well balanced diets. In addition to the tip sheets and classes, NEED also hosts two free doctor and dental workshops each year that allows for individuals that may not have access to health or dental care to be educated and even assisted by health care professionals.

Eating

The NEED program works to instill healthier dietary habits to the community by offering fresh produce to be taken to homes in addition to the weekly dinners. The additional fresh food offered each week allows individuals to supplement the groceries already purchased to create more consistent well balanced meals.

All of what we do at Green Street Catering is completed by volunteers, individuals who believe in our purpose and function and desire to make better the lives of their neighbors in need.

We have a diverse group of volunteers who labor dilligently, care deeply, love unconditionally and laugh loudly. We are a close knit, but open and accepting group who pray together for one another and others and grow close personal relationships that are long lasting.

Our volunteers serve a wide variety of roles including food preparation, door to door delivery, clean up, food pickup and logistics, fund raising and administrative duties, counseling, and praying. If any of these types of roles interest you and you or your organization would be interested in working alongside Green Street, please use the form at the bottom of this page to contact us. We look forward to hearing from you.

Our Team

Green Street Catering began in 2010 with four volunteers. Our team has grown to hundreds of people that serve throughout each week, doing everything from collecting/hauling food donations to food preparation/packaging to food deliver/distributions.
