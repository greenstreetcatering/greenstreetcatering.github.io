Nutrition Education

Green Street's main focus, the desire that drives us, is to provide an opportunity for a hot meal, to anyone who it could benefit.

Green Street offers a delicious, hot, home-cooked meal on Thursday evenings, to any and all that would like one, regardless of means or circumstance. In addition, Green Street works alongside other local church groups and organization to enhance their own service programs, and handling the burden of food preparation, allowing the group to focus on meeting the other physical, emotional and spiritual needs of those they are intent on serving.

Eating

Coupled nicely with Food, Green Street also desires to increase the overall wellness and nutrition of all those we serve. Much more than just a single meal each week, this need requires that we reach into the lives of those that we serve, looking for proactive ways that we can contribute to their overall wellbeing, nutritionally, physically, emotionally and spritually.

This desire led to the formation of our NEED program. NEED stands for "Nutrition, Education, Eating and Diet." This is a program that finds intentional ways that we can bring together our neighbors at Green Street with area healthcare professionals, other service organizations, churhces, and social services.

Diet

All of what we do at Green Street Catering is completed by volunteers, individuals who believe in our purpose and function and desire to make better the lives of their neighbors in need.

We have a diverse group of volunteers who labor dilligently, care deeply, love unconditionally and laugh loudly. We are a close knit, but open and accepting group who pray together for one another and others and grow close personal relationships that are long lasting.

Our volunteers serve a wide variety of roles including food preparation, door to door delivery, clean up, food pickup and logistics, fund raising and administrative duties, counseling, and praying. Beginnings

Started in 2010, we've been busy. Please enjoy a snapshot of what keeps us busy.

  • October 23, 2010

    Discovering a need.

    Green Street was started in 2010 by a group of friends, comfortable in the kitchen, desiring to use their gifts and abilities to provide weekly meals to anyone in need. The program began after the economy had recently taken a downturn and the need for a substantial, warm meal was becoming more and more prevalent. Green Street was initially modeled after a ministry that had been running for many years through a church in NC. After much research and lots of prayer, the friends were confident that a similar program would benefit the High Country. Since then, Green Street Catering has been adapting to the unique needs of the community as they grow and change.

  • Summer 2011

    Neighbors helping neighbors

    Green Street's first meals were served on October 23, 2010. 42 meals were prepared and distributed from the kitchen at Alliance Bible Fellowship in Boone, North Carolina. Many who joined for dinner were requesting additional meals for others, unable to make it to the facility themselves. This began the beauty of people helping people. Over the years, hundreds of community members have become Green Street volunteers by distributing meals and allowing this program to have an even farther reach and benefit. Green Street has served over 70,000 meals since 2010 and the very nature of our organization creates a space for anyone who is willing to take part in serving each other. Green Street Catering is built upon neighbors helping neigbors.

  • July 2013

    A catering company.

    Just as individuals/families are helped by meals, Green Street became aware of many other churches, service organizations and non-profits that are burdened by food costs and food preparation. This began works to cater for company/group gathers so as to curb costs and allow them to focus on accomplishing their specific missions. Green Street desires the success of all community servants. Our partnership through food, allows a tool to strengthen their own programs and purposes. These partnerships have also allowed us the honor and privledge to serve school communities, church groups, hospice patients, those with special needs, veterans, victims of abuse, foster children and families, those with disabilities and sports teams. We cater from small individually plated meals to large buffet affairs. As we all come together, we create stronger individuals and communities.

Weekly community meals

Every Thursday, Green Street Catering offers a free community meal to anyone that it can benefit. Our team of volunteers prepares and delivers an average of 400 meals each week. Since October 2010, we have served and delivered over 70,000 meals.

Green Street works hard throughout the week to gather fresh foods from generous business/organizations. These fresh fruits, vegitables, breads and even desserts are organized and prepared into a well-balanced and nutritious meal every Thursday. Some individuals pick up meals for themselves. Many meals are delivered to homes by partnering churches, small groups and even individuals who also receive meals for themselves and their own families.

People come from all different backgrounds to serve the needs of the community. Likewise, people come from all different backgrounds seeking a hot and healthy meal. Green Street Catering is a place for people helping people.

Holiday Food Baskets

Holidays can be a very tough time for many families. Green Street Catering works to shoudler some of the burden during those times with family food baskets. In 2014, Green Street Catering distributed over 500 food baskets to North Carolina families during the Thanksgiving holiday. Baskets of turkeys/hams and all the trimmings, as well as pounds of staple items, lift food burdens and equip families to enjoy holiday meal traditions, regardless of circumstances.

Through school social workers, church leaders, social services as well as service organization leaders, Green Street Catering provides over 800 food baskets each year. With 50% of North Carolina students participating in school, food service programs, holidays are especially vulnerable periods for these families. Green Street Catering distributes food baskets each Thanksgiving and Easter to offer more security during the holidays.

In 2014, Green Street Catering hosted "packing parties". This gave over 75 volunteers the opportunity to assemble food baskets for others. These parties quickened and lightened the process of packing hundreds of food baskets while offering great food, fun and friendship.
